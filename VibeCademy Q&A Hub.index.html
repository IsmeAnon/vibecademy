Simple answers. No jargon. Just help.

— Buddy & Nova, VibeCademy team
1What is vibe coding?
Vibe coding is when you share your idea in plain English, and the AI builds it for you. No code required.
2Do I need to know coding?
No. You describe what you want; the AI writes the code.
3Do I need a technical background?
Not at all. Vibe coding was made for people with ideas, not for programmers.
4How do I start with an idea?
Just say what you want in simple words. Example: “I want a page where people can sign up for my newsletter.”
5What can I build with it?
Almost anything you can describe: family pages, recipe books, to‑do lists, birthday reminders, small shops, and more.
6What tools do I need?

To try, just go to Lovable.dev or Bolt.new in your browser. No install. For bigger projects, you can use Cursor (free).

📝 Note: Lovable asks for a free account to save your work. Sign up with Google or email – it takes 30 seconds.

📸 This is Lovable’s homepage. Click the link above, sign up, then type your idea and click “Build”.
7Is it free?
Yes, most tools have a free plan to start. You can build your first projects without spending money.
8Do I need to know how the back‑end works?
No. The AI handles the behind‑the‑scenes parts (databases, servers, etc.). You just describe what you want the tool to do.
9What’s the difference between giving instructions and sharing an idea?
Instructions tell the AI how to do something. Ideas tell the AI what you want to achieve. With an idea, the AI can choose the best way to make it happen.
10Where do I start?
Start with a simple idea. Write it in one sentence. Go to Lovable.dev or Bolt.new, paste your idea, and click “Build”. You’ll see your first creation in seconds.
11What AI tools can I use for vibe coding?
Many: DeepSeek (where Nova lives), ChatGPT, Claude, Cursor, Lovable, Bolt.new, and more. You don’t need all of them – just pick one that feels right.
12What if I make a mistake?
Mistakes are part of learning. Nothing breaks permanently. Use the Undo button, or simply tell the AI “go back” or “try again”. With determination, you’ll reach your goal.
13Can I share what I build?
Yes! Most tools give you a link you can send to family, friends, or anyone. Sharing is part of the joy.
14Is this only for young people?
No. Vibe coding is for everyone – from 8 to 80+. If you have an idea, you can build it.
15How long does it take to build something?
Your first simple project can take less than 5 minutes. As you grow, you’ll build more complex things at your own pace.
16Do I need to install anything to start?
Not for your first try. Use a browser‑based tool like Lovable or Bolt.new. No installation, no accounts needed (though Lovable does ask for a free sign‑up to save your work).
